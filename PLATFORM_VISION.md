## 1. Executive Summary

This platform is the financial and operational infrastructure layer for global B2B manufacturing trade — starting with fashion and textiles.

Today, importers manage suppliers over WhatsApp. Factories get paid months after delivery. Buyers discover factories through agents who charge 5–15% commission. Compliance is a manual, paper-heavy exercise. Banks refuse to finance SMB trade because they're blind to what happens inside an order.

This platform solves all of it — in one place.

By combining a **verified factory marketplace**, **order management and TNA tracking**, **embedded supply chain finance**, and **compliance infrastructure**, the platform becomes the operating system that every importer and factory runs their business on.

The model starts in textiles — the most fragmented, most underdigitized, most compliance-pressured B2B sourcing vertical — and expands systematically into furniture, electronics, agri-commodities, and pharmaceuticals.

> **"We are not building a garment ERP. We are building the trust, finance, and compliance infrastructure for global B2B sourcing — starting with textiles as our wedge."**

## 2. The Problem

### For Importers (Buyers)
- Factory discovery is done through agents, trade shows, and personal networks — slow, expensive, and unverified
- No real-time visibility into production — delays are discovered after they've already happened
- Managing 15–20 orders across multiple factories requires stitching together spreadsheets, WhatsApp, email, and 4 different software tools
- EU Digital Product Passport compliance (mandatory from 2027) has no affordable solution for mid-market brands
- Losing a season due to a missed delivery date can cost millions

### For Factories
- Persistent cash flow gap — need money upfront for fabric and workers, but get paid 60–90 days later
- Banks refuse to lend because they don't understand the industry or have visibility into order health
- No platform to showcase verified capabilities to international buyers
- Dependent on agents and middlemen for buyer access

### For the Industry
- $400B+ in fashion sourcing runs on trust that doesn't exist digitally
- No single platform owns the full workflow from factory discovery to payment settlement
- Banks and trade finance firms underwrite blind — resulting in high rates, slow approvals, and frequent rejections for SMBs

## 3. The Solution

### Three Layers, One Platform

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LAYER 3 — COMPLIANCE                                           │
│  Digital Product Passports · ESG Scores · Audit Tracking       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 2 — EMBEDDED FINANCE                                     │
│  Supply Chain Finance · Escrow · Buyer Credit · Trade Insurance │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 1 — MARKETPLACE + ERP                                    │
│  Factory Discovery · RFQ · TNA Tracking · Order Management      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Layer 1 — Verified Factory Marketplace + ERP
- Importers post RFQs. AI matches them to verified factories based on capability, capacity, certifications, and performance history
- Every confirmed order runs on a live TNA (Time and Action) calendar with milestone tracking, document uploads, and buyer confirmations
- Real-time risk scoring detects delays 3–4 weeks before they become crises

### Layer 2 — Embedded Supply Chain Finance
- Factories access working capital financing against confirmed purchase orders
- Buyers benefit from extended payment terms without factories bearing the cost
- All underwriting powered by live TNA data, milestone verifications, and platform-native performance scores — not just invoices
- Escrow model holds buyer funds securely until confirmed delivery

### Layer 3 — Compliance Infrastructure
- Auto-generated Digital Product Passports (EU DPP compliant, mandatory 2027)
- Factory audit and certification tracking
- ESG and carbon footprint reporting
- Traceability from raw material to final delivery

## 4. How It Works — End to End Case Study

### The Characters
- **Sarah** — Owner of a mid-size women's fashion brand in London. $8M/year in retail, 15–20 styles per season. Too complex for spreadsheets, not big enough for a sourcing agent.
- **Raza** — Factory owner in Karachi, Pakistan. 200 machines. WRAP certified. Running at 65% capacity. Needs consistent international orders and timely payments.

---

### Phase 1 — Discovery

Sarah logs into the platform and posts an RFQ:

```
Product      : Linen co-ord set (top + trouser)
Quantity     : 5,000 units
Target FOB   : $12–14/piece
Delivery     : June 15, 2026
Requirements : OEKO-TEX, WRAP preferred
```

The AI surfaces the top 5 factory matches based on:
- Linen/woven production capability
- Available capacity in the Feb–May window
- On-time delivery rate and quality rejection history
- Certification match
- Price range compatibility

Raza's factory appears as Match #2. His verified profile shows:
- 94% on-time delivery rate
- 1.2% quality rejection rate
- WRAP certified, OEKO-TEX in progress
- 40% capacity free in the required window

Sarah requests quotes from 3 factories. Raza responds at $13.20 FOB. She confirms the order on-platform.

---

### Phase 2 — Order Execution

A TNA is auto-generated with milestone dates:

```
Mar 1   → Fabric approval
Mar 10  → Lab dip approval
Mar 20  → Pre-production sample dispatched
Apr 1   → PP sample approved by buyer
Apr 10  → Bulk fabric in-house
May 1   → Bulk production complete
May 15  → AQL Inspection
May 20  → Shipment booking confirmed
Jun 1   → Ex-factory
Jun 15  → Estimated port arrival UK
```

Each milestone requires documentary proof (photos, approvals, reports) before it can be marked complete. Key milestones require buyer confirmation.

**Week 4:** Platform detects fabric approval is 5 days behind schedule. Risk score moves Green → Amber. Sarah receives an early warning notification with a suggested action. She responds immediately — problem caught 6 weeks before it would have become a crisis.

---

### Phase 3 — Supply Chain Finance

It is April 10th. Bulk production is starting. Raza needs:
- Fabric supplier payment: $28,000
- Worker wages (6 weeks): $18,000
- **Total required now: $46,000**

Sarah's payment terms are 30% on shipment, 70% on delivery. Raza won't see real money until June.

Raza applies for Early Payment financing on the platform. The underwriting engine evaluates in real time:

```
✓ PO confirmed by verified buyer
✓ TNA: 8/10 milestones on track
✓ Raza's history: 94% on-time, 0 disputes in 18 months
✓ Order value: $66,000
✓ Platform risk score: LOW
✓ Mandatory inspection booked for May 15

→ Approved: $46,000 advance, disbursed in 3 tranches
→ Finance fee: 2.2%
→ Raza receives first tranche within 48 hours
```

When Sarah pays at shipment and delivery, the platform automatically recovers the advance and fee.

---

### Phase 4 — Compliance

Goods arrive in the UK on June 15th. The platform auto-generates a Digital Product Passport:

```
Style           : Linen Co-ord Set SS26
Fabric Origin   : 100% European Linen, sourced Portugal
Manufacturer    : Raza Garments, Karachi (WRAP Cert #4821)
Carbon Estimate : 3.2 kg CO2 per unit
Certifications  : OEKO-TEX 108334
Traceability    : Full (fabric mill → factory → shipment → buyer)
```

This QR-coded passport ships with every garment — fulfilling EU DPP obligations and enabling brand sustainability marketing.

---

### Phase 5 — The Flywheel

After 3 successful orders, Raza's platform trust score improves. His financing rates drop from 2.2% to 1.8%. He gets surfaced to more buyers. His factory fills up.

Sarah never contacts another factory. Her entire order history, TNA data, finance records, and compliance documents live on the platform. The switching cost is not one product — it is four.

## 5. Business Model & Revenue

### Revenue Streams

**1. Commission on Contracts**
When a buyer and factory confirm an order through the platform, a commission is charged on the total contract value.
```
Rate          : 2–4% of contract value
Example       : $66,000 order at 3% = $1,980
Earned when   : Contract confirmed on-platform
```

**2. Supply Chain Finance Fee**
Charged on the amount financed per transaction.
```
Rate          : 1.5–3.5% per transaction (tiered by factory trust level)
Example       : $46,000 financed at 2.2% = $1,012
Earned when   : Finance facility repaid
Phase 1       : Origination fee shared with NBFC partner
Phase 2       : Full margin retained (own balance sheet)
```

**3. SaaS Subscriptions**
Monthly platform subscriptions for importers and factories.
```
Importers     : $300–800/month (based on order volume)
Factories     : $100–300/month
```

**4. Compliance Module**
EU Digital Product Passport, audit tracking, ESG reporting.
```
Rate          : $200–500/month per brand
Trigger       : EU DPP compliance mandatory from 2027
```

## 6. Platform Cashflow

### Phase 1 — Asset-Light (Years 1–2)

Do not lend platform capital. Partner with an NBFC or trade finance firm.

```
Platform role   : Originator, underwriter, and data provider
Funder role     : NBFC provides capital, takes credit risk
Platform earns  : Origination fee (0.8–1.2% of financed amount)
Benefit         : No capital deployment required, cashflow positive faster
```

**Cashflow in Phase 1:**
```
IN   → SaaS fees (monthly, high margin, recurring)
IN   → Contract commissions (per deal, scales with GMV)
IN   → Origination fees (per financed transaction)
OUT  → Engineering, operations, sales, infrastructure
```

### Phase 2 — Balance Sheet Lending (Years 3+)

Once default rates are proven low across hundreds of transactions, raise a **debt credit facility** (not equity) from a bank or debt fund.

```
Raise           : $5–10M credit facility
Deploy          : Lend directly to factories
Earn            : Full 2–2.5% finance fee (not split with NBFC)
Repay           : Credit facility at ~8–10% annual interest
Net margin      : ~12–15% annual return on deployed capital
```

Equity investors fund the platform and growth. Debt investors fund the loan book. These are separate pools — the standard fintech architecture used by Drip Capital, Karbon Card, and Credable.

### Growth Trajectory

```
Year 1   Build product, acquire first 30 importers. Burn VC capital.
Year 2   Commission + SaaS covers operating costs. Break-even approaching.
Year 3   Finance partnerships generating origination fees. GMV growing.
Year 4   Raise debt facility. Lending directly. Finance margin 3x.
Year 5   $100M+ GMV. Series B on the strength of GMV growth and finance book.
```

## 7. Trust & Data Integrity

### The Problem
If a factory can manipulate milestone data freely, the risk engine becomes unreliable and the finance product becomes dangerous.

### The Solution: 6-Layer Verification Architecture

**Layer 1 — Proof of Completion**
No milestone can be marked complete without documentary evidence:

```
Milestone                Required Upload
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fabric approved          Fabric swatch photo + buyer sign-off
PP sample complete       Sample photos (GPS-tagged, timestamped)
PP sample approved       Buyer approval confirmation (in-platform)
Bulk production done     Packing list + production completion photos
Inspection passed        AQL report from third-party inspector
Shipment booked          Booking confirmation from freight forwarder
```

Photos taken inside the platform app are GPS-tagged and timestamped — impossible to fake without physical presence at the factory.

**Layer 2 — Buyer Confirmation on Key Milestones**
Factory marks complete → buyer receives notification → buyer confirms or disputes within 48 hours. Two-sided verification. Factory cannot fake buyer actions.

**Layer 3 — Third-Party Inspection (Mandatory for Financed Orders)**
Platform integrates directly with QIMA, SGS, Bureau Veritas, or Intertek. Inspection reports feed directly from the inspector's system into the platform — bypassing the factory entirely. The factory never touches that data.

**Layer 4 — Anomaly Detection**
AI flags suspicious behavioral patterns:
```
· 6 milestones logged in a single day after weeks of silence
· Progress jumps from 20% to 90% overnight
· Every milestone completed exactly on due date (statistically unlikely)
· Upload metadata GPS location doesn't match factory address
· Same device logging across multiple separate factory accounts
```

**Layer 5 — Tiered Trust System**

```
Level       Orders    Finance Limit   Inspection Requirement   Finance Fee
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unverified  0         None            All milestones           N/A
Bronze      1–3       $12,000         All milestones           3.5%
Silver      4–10      $50,000         Key milestones           2.5%
Gold        10+       $150,000+       Anomaly-triggered        1.8%
```

**Layer 6 — Skin in the Game**
Factories applying for finance agree to a performance bond (1–2% of financed amount) held in escrow. Data manipulation discovered → bond forfeited + account suspended. Behavioral deterrent with real financial consequence.

---

### Buyer Trustworthiness Assessment

**At Onboarding:**
```
Signal                                  Purpose
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Business registration + VAT/tax ID      Verified legal entity
Bank statements (6 months)              Cash flow health
Credit bureau check (Experian/D&B)      External credit history
Trade references from suppliers         Industry reputation
KYC on company directors                Beneficial ownership
```

**Ongoing Score Inputs:**
- Payment speed vs. agreed terms
- Dispute frequency and outcomes
- Milestone confirmation responsiveness
- Order cancellation rate

**Buyer-Anchored Financing:**
When a buyer is established and verified on the platform, a new factory can still access financing. The buyer's creditworthiness partially underwrites the order — because the risk is on the buyer paying, not solely on the factory delivering.

**Dynamic Payment Terms by Trust Level:**

```
Level   Orders    Escrow Required   Terms Available
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
New     0         100% upfront      Net-0 only
Bronze  1–3       70% upfront       Net-15
Silver  4–10      50% upfront       Net-30
Gold    10+       Escrow optional   Net-45/60
```

**Escrow Model:**
Buyer deposits payment into platform-held escrow before factory ships. Factory ships knowing funds are secured. Platform releases on confirmed delivery. Neither party can defraud the other. Funds in escrow for 60–90 days generate float income for the platform.

## 8. Risk Management Framework

### Worst Case Scenarios & Coverage

**WC1 — Buyer and Factory Collude to Defraud the Platform**

*Scenario:* Factory logs fake milestones, buyer confirms them, platform releases finance. No goods produced. Both disappear.

Coverage:
- Mandatory third-party inspection for every financed order — platform-assigned, not buyer/factory chosen
- New buyer + new factory pairing triggers maximum scrutiny and lowest finance limit
- Anomaly detection flags if buyer confirms milestones suspiciously fast
- Final finance tranche released only after inspection report — never on milestone confirmation alone
- KYC on both parties makes this a criminal matter, not just civil
- Fraud insurance backstop

---

**WC2 — Mass Buyer Default During Economic Downturn**

*Scenario:* Recession. Multiple buyers unable to pay simultaneously. Loan book turns bad at scale.

Coverage:
- Escrow model means buyer funds already held before shipment — most exposure pre-collected
- Trade credit insurance (Euler Hermes/Atradius) covers buyer insolvency
- Hard cap: no single buyer exceeds 8% of total financed GMV
- Geographic diversification across UK, EU, US, Middle East
- Conservative loan-to-value — never finance 100% of order value
- Phased disbursement means exposure at any point is a fraction of total order value

---

**WC3 — Geopolitical Event Disrupts a Sourcing Country**

*Scenario:* Instability, sanctions, or conflict in Pakistan, Bangladesh, or another major hub. Orders cannot complete.

Coverage:
- Force majeure clauses in all platform contracts
- Political risk insurance (Lloyd's of London syndicates)
- Factory network spread across minimum 4 countries — no single country exceeds 40% of active orders
- Platform monitors geopolitical signals and flags at-risk orders automatically
- Contingency matching — platform surfaces alternative factories for incomplete orders immediately

---

**WC4 — Platform Data Breach**

*Scenario:* Platform hacked. Buyer financial data, KYC documents, order history exposed.

Coverage:
- ISO 27001 certification before first paying customer
- End-to-end encryption at rest and in transit
- Role-based access — no factory can see another factory's data
- Quarterly penetration testing by external firm
- Cyber liability insurance
- GDPR compliance built into architecture from Day 1
- 72-hour breach notification protocol

---

**WC5 — Regulatory Change Restricts the Finance Product**

*Scenario:* A jurisdiction changes fintech lending regulations, requiring a banking license or banning the model.

Coverage:
- Phase 1 asset-light model — NBFC partner holds the license, not the platform
- Platform's core value (ERP + marketplace + data) survives even if finance is restricted
- SaaS + commission revenue continues regardless
- Finance product structured as "facilitation" not "lending" in early stages
- Legal counsel monitoring regulatory changes per jurisdiction

---

**WC6 — Quality Disaster, Buyer Refuses Payment**

*Scenario:* Factory delivers 30% defective units. Buyer rejects shipment. Factory cannot return the advance.

Coverage:
- AQL inspection before shipment — final tranche only released after inspection passes
- If inspection fails, shipment held until rework is completed
- Buyer's escrow not released until acceptable delivery confirmed
- Factory performance bond covers partial recovery
- Platform audit trail (photos, inspection reports) makes fraudulent disputes difficult

---

**WC7 — Shipping Crisis or Cargo Loss**

*Scenario:* Global shipping disruption. Goods lost, damaged, or stuck at ports for months.

Coverage:
- Cargo insurance mandatory for all financed orders — bundled into platform service
- Marine insurance covers loss and damage in transit
- Finance repayment tied to delivery confirmation, not shipment date
- Force majeure provisions extend repayment terms automatically for documented disruptions
- Platform monitors major shipping route disruptions and flags at-risk orders

---

**WC8 — Customer Concentration Risk**

*Scenario:* Top 5 buyers represent 50% of GMV. Two leave or go bust. Revenue collapses.

Coverage:
- Hard cap: no single buyer exceeds 8% of platform GMV
- No single country exceeds 35% of buyer-side GMV
- Stickiness through data — switching means losing order history, factory relationships, trust scores, compliance records
- Long-term contracts with anchor buyers (annual SaaS + volume commitments)
- Dedicated new buyer acquisition budget

---

## 9. Disintermediation Risk & Platform Retention

### The Risk

After 2–3 successful orders, a factory owner calls the buyer directly:

> "Let's cut the platform. I'll give you 3% off — same saving as the commission. We both win."

This is called disintermediation — the single biggest structural risk in any marketplace business. The answer is not policing users. It is making the platform worth more than the commission.

---

### The Finance Cost Misconception

A common assumption: if the platform charges a finance fee, the factory still pays interest — so what's the benefit over going direct?

The real comparison:

```
On-Platform Finance
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finance fee (2.2% on $46,000)     :  $1,012
Approval time                     :  48 hours
Collateral required               :  None
Eligibility                       :  Guaranteed if milestones verified
Rate basis                        :  Platform-negotiated bulk NBFC rate

Factory Approaches Bank Directly
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Interest (15–25% annual, 90 days) :  $1,610–$2,760
Processing fees                   :  $200–500
Approval time                     :  3–6 weeks
Collateral required               :  Property, machinery, personal assets
Eligibility                       :  Often rejected entirely for SMBs
```

**The platform does not eliminate the NBFC cost. It makes it cheaper, faster, and accessible to factories that banks would otherwise reject.**

### Why the Platform Gets Better Rates Than Any Factory Can Alone

A single factory approaching an NBFC independently has no performance data, no verified buyer relationship, and high perceived risk. The platform approaches the same NBFC with hundreds of verified factory profiles, live TNA milestone data, confirmed purchase orders, and a portfolio-level default history.

The NBFC gives the platform an institutional bulk rate because the risk is demonstrably lower and the volume is guaranteed. No individual factory can negotiate that. This is the same reason a staffing agency gets better insurance rates than a single freelancer.

### The True Cost Breakdown

```
Factory finances $46,000 on-platform
  Finance fee (2.2%)     →  $1,012
  Of which:
    NBFC interest         →  ~$650 (cost of capital + credit risk)
    Platform margin       →  ~$362 (origination + service fee)

Factory goes to bank directly
  Interest (15% annual, 90 days)   →  $1,725
  Processing and documentation fees →  $200–500
  Total cost                        →  $1,925–$2,225+
  Plus: production delayed 3–6 weeks waiting for approval
  Plus: possibly rejected entirely
```

Platform finance costs $1,012. Bank costs $1,925+. And the bank might say no.

---

### The Full Retention Architecture

Finance alone is one layer of retention — not the only one. The complete picture:

**Lock 1 — Finance Only Available On-Platform**
Raza's credit limit, financing rate, and Gold trust score exist only within the platform. Going direct means losing access to working capital entirely — or paying significantly more for it elsewhere.

**Lock 2 — Escrow Only Covers On-Platform Orders**
Sarah's payment is only protected if the transaction is confirmed on-platform. A direct wire transfer has no escrow, no dispute resolution, no insurance, and no legal recourse through the platform. No rational buyer accepts that risk on a $66,000 order.

**Lock 3 — Contact Details Are Never Fully Exposed**
Factory profiles display capability, certifications, and performance scores — not direct phone numbers, personal email, or WhatsApp. All communication runs through the platform's internal messaging system until a contract is confirmed. By that point the transaction is already on-platform.

**Lock 4 — Compliance Tied to On-Platform Orders**
Digital Product Passports are only generated for orders that flow through the platform. From 2027, EU DPP compliance is legally mandatory for every garment sold in Europe. An off-platform order produces no DPP — making the buyer non-compliant regardless of what the factory provides.

**Lock 5 — Non-Circumvention Clause**
Standard legal protection used by Upwork, Toptal, and every serious marketplace. If a buyer and factory who connected through the platform transact directly within 24 months, a circumvention fee equal to 3x the standard commission is immediately payable by both parties. Legally enforceable. Creates real financial and legal risk for anyone who tries.

**Lock 6 — The Reputation Economy**
Raza's Gold trust score is his most valuable business asset — took 10 orders and 18 months to build. It determines his ranking in buyer search results, his financing limit and rate, and the quality of buyers routed to him. Account suspension means starting over at Bronze with a $12,000 limit and mandatory inspections on every milestone. No commission saving justifies that loss.

**Lock 7 — Direct Pair Pricing**
Rather than pretending the relationship doesn't exist after multiple orders, acknowledge it and price it differently. After 3+ completed orders between the same pair, offer a Direct Pair Plan:

```
Standard commission (new matches)      →  3%
Direct Pair Plan (established pairs)   →  1%

Platform still provides:
  · TNA tracking and milestone management
  · Finance and working capital
  · Escrow and payment settlement
  · Compliance and DPP generation
  · Dispute resolution
```

This removes the incentive to circumvent by reducing the commission — while retaining the relationship, the data, and all high-margin services.

### Why Escrow Is a Cost Advantage, Not a Cost Burden

The comparison is not escrow vs. nothing. It is escrow vs. what international trade currently uses — the Letter of Credit (LC).

**What a Letter of Credit Costs Today**

When Sarah places a $66,000 order with Raza without any platform, Raza will not ship without financial security. The industry standard is an LC issued by the buyer's bank.

```
Letter of Credit — What It Costs Sarah
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LC issuance fee (buyer's bank)   :  0.5–2% = $330–$1,320
Amendment fees (per change)      :  $50–150 each
Confirmation fee (factory's bank):  0.5–1% if required
Documentation and handling fees  :  $100–300
Time to arrange                  :  2–4 weeks
Reduces Sarah's bank credit line :  Yes
Total cost per order             :  $600–$2,000+
```

After all that, an LC is still paper-heavy, prone to documentation discrepancies, and requires lawyers when disputed.

**What Platform Escrow Costs**

```
Platform Escrow
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wire transfer / settlement fee   :  $25–50 flat (or 0.3% = $198)
Time to arrange                  :  Instant — collected digitally
Requires bank relationship       :  No
Reduces bank credit line         :  No
Total cost per order             :  $50–200
```

**The Saving Per Order**

```
LC cost              :  $600–$2,000+
Platform escrow      :  $50–200
──────────────────────────────────────
Sarah saves          :  $400–$1,800 per order
```

Sarah gets a better product — digital, instant, with a built-in dispute resolution mechanism and full audit trail — at a fraction of what she was already paying.

**Three Reasons Escrow Becomes a Revenue Stream**

*1. Float Income*

Sarah deposits $66,000 into platform escrow. That money sits for 60–90 days before being released to Raza. At scale:

```
$10M held in escrow at any time
Invested at 4–5% annually (money market / T-bills)
= $400,000–$500,000 per year in passive interest income
```

This is how Airbnb generates hundreds of millions annually — they hold guest payments before releasing to hosts. The transfer cost of $40 per order becomes completely irrelevant.

*2. It Replaces a Cost Both Parties Already Had*

Sarah was already paying $600–$2,000 per order in LC fees. Raza was already waiting 2–4 weeks for LC processing before he could start production. Platform escrow eliminates both pains at a fraction of the cost. Neither party feels the escrow fee — they feel the saving.

*3. It Unlocks Orders That Would Never Have Happened*

Many mid-market importers avoid LCs because they reduce available bank credit limits. With platform escrow, no bank involvement is required at all. Orders that were previously blocked by banking constraints now flow through the platform — generating commission, finance, and compliance revenue that would not have existed otherwise.

**Summary**

```
Traditional LC       :  $600–$2,000+, 2–4 weeks, paper-heavy, reduces bank credit
Platform Escrow      :  $50–200, instant, digital, no bank required
Float income (scale) :  $400K–$500K/year

Escrow is not a fee the platform charges.
It is a fee the platform eliminates — and earns from in the process.
```

## 10. Market Expansion Strategy

### Why Not Textile-Only

The core model — verified supplier marketplace, order management, embedded finance, compliance — is the same problem across dozens of B2B sourcing industries. The textile-specific components (TNA templates, certification types, compliance frameworks) are configurable, not hardcoded.

Starting in textiles is a strategic wedge, not a permanent ceiling.

### Key Differentiators vs. Each Competitor

**vs. Alibaba / Global Sources**
- Alibaba gets you to the factory gate. This platform manages everything after — and verifies the factory is real before you get there. No TNA, no finance, no compliance on Alibaba.

**vs. Faire**
- Faire connects brands to retailers (finished goods). This platform connects importers to factories (manufacturing). Different customer, different supply chain stage, higher complexity, larger order values.

**vs. Anvyl**
- Anvyl is a visibility tool. No marketplace, no finance, no compliance. You still need to find your own factories and stitch together your own financing. Pure SaaS with no transaction revenue model.

**vs. Fast React / Coats Digital**
- Enterprise-only. Minimum 6-month implementation. Built for billion-dollar brands. This platform serves the 50,000 mid-market importers they've never touched, with zero implementation cost and immediate value.

**vs. Drip Capital**
- Drip lends against invoices. This platform lends against live, verified production data. Underwriting is fundamentally more accurate because the order lifecycle is visible from Day 1 — not just at invoice stage. Lower default risk, better pricing.

**vs. Inspectorio / QIMA**
- Point solutions that need to be bolted on to something else. This platform integrates with them as a data feed — they become inputs into the risk engine, not competitors.

**vs. TextileGenesis / TrusTrace / Sourcemap**
- Compliance-only tools that require manual data entry. This platform generates compliance data natively as a byproduct of normal order management. No separate exercise required.
